Hi, I'm Poruri Sai Rahul, an undergraduate at the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras. I'm currently pursuing a BS & MS degree in Physics.My interests lie in astronomy, astrophysics and instrumentation.

I did my schooling in Ongole, Andhra Pradesh and my +2 at Narayana Junior College, Hyderabad. After being coached/trained to write IIT-JEE for 2 years, I finally made it through with a rank of 2944. I joined IIT Madras to pursue a degree in BS & MS in Physics.

Over the last 5 years, my interests have varied across the extra-curricular & co-curricular activity scene here at IIT Madras.

First year -
  • Volunteer, Shaastra Car Team, Shaastra 2009.
  • Volunteer, Phototography Team, Saarang 2010.
  • Volunteer, Equipment Team, Saarang 2010.
  • Volunteer, Extra Mural Lecture Team 2009-2010, IIT Madras.
  • Volunteer, Literary Secretary Team, Jamuna Hostel, 2009-2010.

Second year -
  • Co-Ordinator, Astronomy Workshop, Shaatra 2010.
  • Core Team Member, Astro IITM.
    - As part of organizational commitee of Astro IITM, I've organized and conducted popular astronomy talks on a topics ranging from 'Scale of the Universe' to 'Evolution of stars' alongside regular observation sessions. We also organized the aforementioned workshop during the technical festival of IIT Madras on the basics of building a telescope. Over a 4 day period, the participants (10 teams of 5) were taught the basics of telescopes at the end of which, they had a 4 inch newtonian telescope with a dobsonian mount.


Third year -
  • Co-Ordinator, Astronomy Workshop, Shaastra 2011.
  • Core Team Member, Astro IITM.
    - As part of organizational commitee of Astro IITM, I've conducted and spoken at outreach sessions to schools in Chennai where high school children were introduced to the basics of astronomy, telescopes and sky watching using telescopes. We also organized the aforementioned workshop during Shaastra on Astrophotography. Dr. Suresh Mohan, a prominent astrophotographer, introduced the basics of astrophotography & CCDs and post-processing of astronomical images using photoshop/gimp.
